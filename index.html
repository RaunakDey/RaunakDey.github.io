Raunak Dey

I am a fourth year Physics PhD student at the Quantitative Viral Dynamics group at the University of Maryland, College Park. I am advised by Prof. Joshua Weitz and Dr. Stephen Beckett. My research is highly interdisciplinary -- I solve problems in Biology, especially viral dynamics, using the tools of Physics (networks, complex systems) and Computer Science (Deep Learning, Bayesian Inference).

The interface between Deep Learning and dynamical systems has a lot of use cases in both--
(i) Forward modeling, i.e., where we find mechanism driven models for multiple viruses and microbes interacting together.
(ii) Inverse modeling, i.e., where we find the posterior distribution of parameters for given data and model.
As virus-microbe communities form complex system with emergent effects, the modeling the inference problems often go on iteratively.
