Raunak Dey

I am a fourth-year Physics Ph.D. student at the Department of Physics at the University of Maryland, College Park. I am advised by Prof. Joshua Weitz and mentored by Dr. Stephen Beckett. My research focuses on solving inverse problems using time series data. Given data points ordered in time, I aim to develop interpretable models and algorithms to infer the underlying phenomena governing the dynamics of the system. To do so, I employ several tools from Bayesian inference, control theory, and optimization.

My research is highly interdisciplinary, at the intersection of Physics, Machine Learning, and Biology. I have extensive professional experience in handling large datasets, developing end-to-end hardware and software pipelines for precision instrumentation, and industrial experience in managing a professional team. Some of my projects include: developing pharmacological inverse models for phage therapy from multimodal datasets, inventing control algorithms for Optical Tweezers-based precision microrheology, creating constrained optimizers for network inference from time-series data, and devising machine learning pipelines for automated noise detection and removal in radio astronomy time series.
